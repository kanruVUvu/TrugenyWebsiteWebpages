Heart disease

Heart disease occurs when plaque develops in the arteries and blood vessels that lead to the heart. This blocks important nutrients and oxygen from reaching your heart. Plaque is a waxy substance made up of cholesterol, fatty molecules, and minerals.

Many heart disorders can run in families, indicating inherited genetic risk factors. When lifestyle factors add on to inherited ones, it increases an individualâ€™s risk for having a cardiac event

Genes can influence your risks for heart disease.

Every aspect of your heart system is controlled by your genes. A single genetic variation in a single gene can affect your risks for developing a heart disease. Genetic variations are passed on from parents to children.

Genetic factors impact every 1 in 200 people. These heart conditions if left undetected can be life- threatening.

With early detection most inherited heart conditions can be managed successfully

Knowing your risk early can help you and your Doctor, to create a personalized plan with regular screenings to prevent heart disease.

Learning about your heart can help your family.

Inherited heart conditions are passed from one generation to the next and run in families. Learning about your risks can help your lfamily take proactive actions to keep their own hearts healthier.
